About the data
What is this counting — and how is it different from an ancestry map?

Only people actually born abroad: the immigrants themselves, as they reported their country of birth to the American Community Survey.

What are the neighborhood shapes?

Neighborhood Tabulation Areas (NTAs): the Department of City Planning's grouping of the city's 2,327 census tracts into named areas of roughly 15,000–50,000 people — small enough to mean something, large enough for reliable survey estimates. NTA boundaries are built from tract math, not lived experience, so they sometimes split what people consider one neighborhood — that's why there's a “Bushwick (West)” and a “Bushwick (East)”, and why the city's Newest New Yorkers report merges the 197 residential NTAs into 164 more relatable combinations.

How reliable are the numbers?

They are survey estimates with sampling error. Margins of error were combined across tracts (root sum of squares); estimates flagged “use with caution” have a coefficient of variation of 12–40%, and “unreliable” above 40%. England, Scotland and the rest of the UK are combined; “China” excludes Hong Kong and Taiwan, which are tabulated separately. Parks, airports and cemeteries are left uncolored.

Why does the city's own report say Chinese immigrants are now the largest group?

The Newest New Yorkers 2026 headline — Chinese immigrants (397,249) overtaking Dominicans (389,779) — uses single-year 2023 estimates and counts “China, Total”, which includes Hong Kong and Taiwan. This map uses the steadier 2019–2023 five-year average, on which the Dominican Republic (402,337) still leads, and lists Hong Kong (34,352) and Taiwan (20,837) separately, as the underlying census table does; combined with mainland China they total 392,910 here — the same neck-and-neck story on a slightly earlier clock. (The report aggregates the same census tracts into the same neighborhood areas used on this map.)

Are the newest arrivals (2022–24) included?

Undercounted. The Census Bureau later revised New York City's mid-2023 population upward by roughly 133,000 — largely asylum seekers — but that correction is not reflected in the 2023 ACS, and the 2019–2023 five-year average used here dilutes the recent influx further. The city's newest communities are larger than this map shows.

What does “signature group” mean?

The community most concentrated in a neighborhood relative to its share of the whole city — a location quotient, the same math search engines use to find the words that distinguish a document. 3× means a community is three times as concentrated there as its citywide norm, which is what makes a small group like Uzbekistan-born Bukharians light up Forest Hills while the citywide giants only register where they truly cluster. Signature picks require at least 400 people and a usable margin of error.
